Lecturers On Strike

In an unexpected turn of events, the Computer Science Department at the University of Ghana has taken a unified stand by initiating a strike. The cause, as speculated among students and faculty, revolves around the alleged non-payment of salaries Lecturers, driven by the need for financial stability, have collectively decided to halt their teaching duties until the issue is resolved. This unforeseen development has left students and the university community awaiting official communication on the matter, hoping for a swift resolution to restore normalcy to the academic environment

UG names Tackie Richmond VP and CIO

Tackie Richmond, the top-ranking information technology administrator at the Mayor Institute of Technology, will become UG’s vice president for information technology and chief information officer on Jan. 3, 2024

He will succeed Paula Otoo, who has served in this role since 2012.

UG Student introduces the first interactive AI

In a groundbreaking achievement, a female student at the University of Ghana, Charlotte Mensah has unveiled the institution's first interactive artificial intelligence system. This innovative creation marks a significant leap forward in the integration of cutting-edge technology within the academic landscape

The AI, developed with a keen focus on user interaction, promises to revolutionize the learning experience for students and faculty alike. Its capabilities range from assisting with coursework and answering queries to providing personalized recommendations for academic and extracurricular activities.

The introduction of this interactive AI not only showcases the prowess of the university's students in the realm of artificial intelligence but also opens doors to a new era of dynamic and engaging educational practices. The campus is abuzz with anticipation as the university embraces the possibilities that this pioneering initiative brings to the forefront of technological advancement

UG updates email forwarding service

Over the course of this upcoming academic year, UG will discontinue its alumni email forwarding service and offer alumni either a free Google Workspace account, allowing them to keep UG email address, or an invitation to update their email address to a preferred one instead.

Why make this change now? “The world and especially the big email providers are clamping down on email authentication,” said Mildred Mami-Tei, director of cloud infrastructure services at UG. “They are less tolerant of emails that appear to be spoofed.”
